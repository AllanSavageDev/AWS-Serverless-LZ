Cloudfront For Global Static Content


Everyone Has Static Content

CloudFront makes it easy to deploy your web content gloabally from a single CloudFront Distribution and all but removes management entirely. Instead of maintaining Nginx and LetsEncrypt certs, a simple S3 bucket hosts your /www securely behind your configured DNS name.

Simply update the S3 bucket that backs your distribution, invalidate the cache and you're deployed globaly from a single action.

Of course there are some complications in linking CloudFront to S3 securely. OAC, REST vs HTTP distro types and of course the needed Lambda Edge function to properly load index files. Once those configuration hurdles are clear, its by and large a done deal about a giant value add.


CloudFront as the Edge Layer

CloudFront isn't just a CDN — it's the edge of your architecture. It terminates HTTPS, handles caching, absorbs traffic spikes, and provides the performance surface that makes your site feel fast anywhere in the world. By fronting your static assets with CloudFront, you remove an entire layer of operational overhead and gain a globally distributed delivery mechanism with effectively no moving parts to maintain.
